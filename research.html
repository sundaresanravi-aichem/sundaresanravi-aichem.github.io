Research & Projects

An overview of my academic research experience and independent projects, focusing on computational material science, theoretical chemistry, and machine learning.

Enhancing Supercapacitive Performance of WS₂ via Transition-Metal Intercalation

Research Intern, SRM Institute of Science and Technology

Supervisor: Dr. Tumpa Sadhukhan, Research Assistant Professor, SRM Institute of Science and Technology

Apr 2025 - Jul 2025

Project Overview & Responsibilities

  • Performed periodic Density Functional Theory (DFT) calculations using VASP to study the structural stability, electronic structure, and energetics of transition-metal-intercalated WS₂ systems.
  • Developed custom Python-based workflows to systematically process density-of-states (DOS) data.
  • Computed quantum capacitance to analyze structure–property relationships critical for supercapacitor performance.

Machine learning for predicting spectroscopic properties of transition-metal complexes

Thesis Extension Project

Supervisor: Dr. Tumpa Sadhukhan, Research Assistant Professor, SRM Institute of Science and Technology

Dec 2025 - Present

An extension of my academic thesis project. Driven by the goal of improving upon initial findings, the project's scope was expanded to develop more robust predictive models.

  • Designed dative-bonded SMILES representations of transition-metal complexes specifically tailored for machine-learning inputs.
  • Built & curated a comprehensive database of transition-metal complexes and performed quantum chemical calculations using Gaussian to extract precise electronic and spectroscopic properties.
  • Prepared datasets and developed computational workflows to train machine-learning models for predicting spectroscopic behavior, with applications relevant to photodynamic therapy.
